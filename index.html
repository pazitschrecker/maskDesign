Goal

Design a COVID mask that people want to wear.

Challenge
Wearing a mask during COVID is the best and easiest way to protect yourself and others during the pandemic. And yet, this practice is far from universal.

One reason is that masks are uncomfortable, unsuitable, restrictive or otherwise undesirable to wear in public. (We are currently not addressing the challenge of people who believe that masks don’t keep you safe).

Designs a mask (or mask enhancement) that will encourage at least 5 more people to wear their mask more, and has comparable safety to existing masks.

Synthesis

Insights

The four primary insights drawn from the background analysis and interviews with participants are the following:

  • Only a select few models, makes and brands of masks were designed to retain their efficacy in varying weather conditions, particularly in rain and snow.
  • People required to use eyeglasses found masks that employ a skeletal wire to maintain their shapes difficult to accommodate on the bridge of the nose, often coming into contact with the frame and creating condensation on the lenses.
  • Interviewees expressed particular importance to the environmental impact of mask-usage, and all opted to use sustainable mask options;
  • Interviewees also noted that, during conversations, masks with lesser material often collapsed into the mouth area, disabling the person from conversing properly; to counter this effect, other mask-wearing participants would partially or fully retract their masks, thus exposing their mouths and noses.
Reframing User Needs

From three identified clusters (namely: weather-effectiveness, comfort for glasses users, and communication challenges), 5 potential pursuable opportunities emerged:

  1. Can a mask be created that maintains its effectiveness in varying weather conditions?
  2. Can a mask be created that protects glass lenses from condensation whilst remaining comfortable on the bridge of the nose?
  3. Can a mask be created that is centered around sustainable practices whilst remaining comfortable and efficacious?
  4. Can mask flexibility be increased to fit different face shapes and sizes?
  5. Can a mask designed around enhancing safe communication be created to counter the need to remove the mask altogether?

Ideate

Brainstorming

Most ideas seeking to address the aforementioned questions centered around modifying the design of the mask.

Addressing the weather challenges:

  • Adding a water-resistant layer to a mask, or designing it with water-resistant materials.
  • Decreasing condensation, heat or sweat through the addition of a (solar-powered) fan on the skirts of the mask.
  • Adding a small platform above the nose to protect against water.
  • Designing awning-like skirts to allow water to roll off masks.

Addressing comfort and efficacy:

  • Combining the comfortable features of surgical masks with cloth materials; for instance, following surgical-design patterns.
  • Designing masks tailored for reusability through daily disinfection with medical products.
  • Creating masks with studier materials to emphasize longevity of use.
  • Devising masks with a self-cleaning apparatus
  • Producing masks with replaceable filters.

Addressing communication challenges:

  • Designing masks with a clear panel around the mouth area, or fully transparent masks.
  • Engineering a mechanism that allows masks to rest without contact with skin.
  • Creating masks with a built-in microphone to address needs by public-speakers.
  • Generating masks based on 3-dimensional modelling of origami.
  • Including a screen-like technology to create real-time captioning, or display emojis, that would rests within the mouth area.
  • Incorporating a discrete camera into mask technology.
  • Creating skin-tight masks to display mouth movements.

Other avenues combining mask technology with external solutions were also considered:

  • Creating environmentally-friendly mask sanitizer, used in a similar fashion to deep-cleaning contact solutions.
  • Inventing a mask-only recycling service with financial incentives to promote sustainability.

Prototype

Sketches

Designs range from simple interventions to existing masks to creative manifestations of ideas from our ideate phase.

There is heavy use of clear material in most of the designs, as the visual cue of facial expressions aid in both verbal and non-verbal communication.

Latter designs combine E-textile technology and mics to enhance the communicative tasks wearers will undergo.

Test

We evaluated the designs through a table with additional criteria the team believe to be important for the mask to embody. A lot of focus is placed on the user experience of the mask: 1) comfort, 2) ease in maintenance, 3) affordability, 4) eco-friendliness. We also want to help those with communication disabilities to benefit from the design. Looking into the colder months, the team believes that a mask should also be water-resistant in the case of snow and rain.

Finalize

Our top three ideas are as follows:

  • Design No. 1 - Plastic bracing: this accessory could be easily mass produced and is affordable. It works for both fabric and disposable masks to stop masks from collapsing when people are talking or breathing in. We chose this for its potential to help the most number of people.
  • Design No. 4 - LED expression mask: the idea requires some technological advancement that is still feasible. This not only opens up the possibility of helping people who use sign language to communicate, but also allows for decorative personalizations on users’ masks.
  • Design No. 6 - Rigid foldable mask: the design is not only easy to maintain and clean, but also allows for people with hearing disabilities to communicate in. It is also affordable and durable.
Solution
